Invention of the Internet!

A Historic revolution in the communication of the human race

Internet’s origin goes back to the 1950’s and has touched so many lives since then. During the Gulf war, when crisis arose to be better informed the U.S. Department of Defense decided to fund ARPANET for their research in this area. At that time, ARPANET used packet switching allowing multiple computers to communicate with each other on a single network. After years of research and work in this field, on October 29, 1969, a first message was delivered from a “node to node” communication from one computer to another by ARPAnet.

During this era, telephone exchange worked differently where a middleman had to connect to callers. Damaged communication became a bottleneck during the Nuclear attacks. Communications became an issue without the middleman connecting the two callers. The telephone exchange wanted to analyze and improve its operations. Research starting to spur around how many circuits would be required to provide a service for people not to be on hold for too long to talk with the person on the other end of the call. Queuing theory was introduced and named after the founder of this theory; the international unit of telephone is called the Erlang. Queuing theory is referred to as the mathematical study of the formation, function, and congestion of waiting lines, or queues.
